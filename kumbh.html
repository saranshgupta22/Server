Kumbh Mela 2025 is not merely a religious celebration but is the ultimate cultural experience, bringing millions together worldwide. Also regarded as the world’s biggest spiritual gathering, the Kumbh fair is one of the spectacular events that congregates faith, history, and tradition all at one time. The dates of the Kumbh Mela are from January to February 2025. How to Reach Kumbh Mela

  1. Bamrauli Airport (Prayagraj) is 12 km from the Kumbh Mela location. Take a flight to Varanasi (120 km).
  2. The primary railway station is Prayagraj Junction, which links you to the major cities. Plan early as tickets are in high demand and the rush is enormous during the Kumbh fair.
  3. Most highways are easily reachable and connect well with various towns from Kumbh such as Kanpur and Varanasi.

Must-Visit Places During Kumbh Mela

  1. Triveni Sangam: The confluence of the Ganga, Yamuna, and Saraswati rivers, this is the holiest spot for pilgrims to take a dip during the Kumbh Mela.
  2. Akshayavat Tree: Known as the "indestructible banyan tree," it is a sacred site with deep mythological significance.
  3. Allahabad Fort: Built by Emperor Akbar, this historic fort offers stunning views of the Sangam and houses the Patalpuri Temple and Saraswati Koop.
  4. Hanuman Mandir: A unique temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman, with an idol in a reclining posture, located near the Sangam.
  5. Alopi Devi Temple: A significant Shakti Peeth, this temple is known for its unique deity, represented by a wooden carriage instead of an idol.

These places offer a mix of spiritual, historical, and cultural experiences, making your visit to the Mahakumbh Mela even more enriching and memorable.
